About Me

I am an assitant professor at the Department of Astronomy and Astrophysics at University of Chicago, and a senior member of the Kavli Institute of Cosmological Physics (KICP).

My science interest is to use large optical imaging and spectroscopic survey data to study the evolution of the recent Universe through a technique called weak gravitational lensing. I'm also interested in combining the information from weak gravitational lensing with other fields such as large-scale structure, the cosmic microwave background, and galaxy evolution. I am currently involved in DES and LSST.

Prior to my current appointment, I was a KICP fellow for two years. Before that, I was a postdoctoral researcher at the Institute of Astronomy at ETH Zurich, in the Cosmology group lead by Prof. Alexandre Refregier. And before that I finished my PhD in Physics at Stanford. My thesis advisors are Prof. Steve Kahn and Prof. Rafe Schindler. I did my undergrad at the the Physics Department at National Taiwan University.

I'm genuinly interested in pretty things --- science, art, nature and the simplicity of life.
